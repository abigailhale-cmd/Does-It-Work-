Blood Type Genetics

Dominant vs. Recessive

Blood type is controlled by alleles. The A (IA) and B (IB) alleles are dominant, while the O allele (i) is recessive. A recessive trait only appears when both alleles are recessive.

Codominance

A and B alleles are codominant. This means that when both are present, they are expressed equally. This results in blood type AB.

Multiple Alleles

Blood type is an example of multiple alleles. There are three possible alleles in the population: IA, IB, and i. Each person inherits two of these alleles.
