Blood Type Genetics

Dominant vs. Recessive

Blood type is determined by alleles. The IA and IB alleles are dominant, while the i allele is recessive. A recessive trait is only expressed when both alleles are recessive.

Codominance

The IA and IB alleles are codominant. When both alleles are present, they are expressed equally, resulting in blood type AB.

Multiple Alleles

Blood type is an example of multiple alleles. There are three alleles in the population: IA, IB, and i. Each person inherits two alleles, one from each parent.
